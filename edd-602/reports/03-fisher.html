Fisher's Formula

Sample Size Estimation Based on Population Proportion

Introduction

Fisher's Formula is a widely accepted statistical method for determining the required sample size when estimating a population proportion. It is commonly used in educational research, public health studies, and survey research where researchers aim to estimate the proportion of individuals possessing a particular characteristic.


Learning Objectives

  • Explain the purpose of Fisher's Formula.
  • Identify the variables used in the formula.
  • Compute the required sample size.
  • Interpret the computed result.

Historical Background

Fisher's Formula is based on statistical principles developed by Sir Ronald A. Fisher, one of the pioneers of modern statistics. The formula is frequently applied when estimating population proportions, especially in large populations.


Definition

Fisher's Formula estimates the minimum sample size required to achieve a specified confidence level and margin of error when estimating a population proportion.


Formula

n = (Z2 * p * q) / d2


Variables

Symbol Description
Z Z-score corresponding to the selected confidence level.
p Estimated population proportion.
q 1 - p.
d Desired margin of error.
n Required sample size.

Common Values

Confidence Level Z Value
90% 1.645
95% 1.96
99% 2.576

Worked Example

Given:
  • Z = 1.96
  • p = 0.50
  • q = 0.50
  • d = 0.05

Step 1

n = (1.962 * 0.50 * 0.50) / 0.052

Step 2

1.962 = 3.8416

Step 3

3.8416 * 0.50 * 0.50 = 0.9604

Step 4

0.052 = 0.0025

Step 5

n = 0.9604 / 0.0025 = 384.16

Required Sample Size = 384 Respondents

Applications

  • Educational surveys
  • Health and medical research
  • Community-based studies
  • Market research
  • Social science research

Advantages

  • Simple and widely accepted.
  • Produces reliable sample size estimates.
  • Suitable for estimating population proportions.
  • Applicable across various research fields.

Limitations

  • Requires an estimate of the population proportion.
  • Assumes random sampling.
  • May require adjustment for finite populations.

Comparison with Cochran Formula

Fisher's Formula Cochran Formula
Uses margin of error represented by d. Uses margin of error represented by e.
Commonly used in health and survey research. Widely applied in educational and social research.
Both estimate sample size using population proportion. Both produce nearly identical results under the same assumptions.

Summary

Fisher's Formula is an effective method for determining sample size when estimating a population proportion. It is particularly useful for surveys involving large populations and provides statistically sound estimates when appropriate values are chosen.
