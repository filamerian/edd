Krejcie and Morgan Formula

Determining Sample Size Using a Standard Reference Table

Introduction

Krejcie and Morgan developed one of the most widely used methods for determining sample size in educational and social science research. Rather than performing lengthy statistical computations, researchers simply refer to the Krejcie and Morgan Sample Size Table to identify the recommended sample size for a known population.


Learning Objectives

  • Explain the purpose of the Krejcie and Morgan Formula.
  • Use the Krejcie and Morgan Table correctly.
  • Interpret the recommended sample size.
  • Identify situations where this method is appropriate.

Historical Background

In 1970, Robert V. Krejcie and Daryle W. Morgan published a sample size table in the journal Educational and Psychological Measurement. Their work became one of the most frequently cited references for determining sample size in educational research.


Definition

The Krejcie and Morgan Formula is used to determine the recommended sample size for a finite population. In practice, researchers usually use the published table instead of calculating the formula manually.


Formula

s = X2 * N * P (1 - P) / d2 (N - 1) + X2 * P (1 - P)


Variables

Symbol Description
s Required sample size
X2 Chi-square value for 1 degree of freedom
N Population size
P Population proportion (usually 0.50)
d Margin of error

Worked Example

Suppose the total population consists of 1,000 teachers.

Looking at the Krejcie and Morgan Table, the recommended sample size is 278 respondents.

Required Sample Size = 278 Respondents

Applications

  • Educational research
  • Graduate thesis and dissertation studies
  • Community surveys
  • Business research
  • Government studies

Advantages

  • Very easy to use.
  • No calculator is required when using the table.
  • Widely accepted in academic research.
  • Suitable for known populations.

Limitations

  • Applicable only to finite populations.
  • Based on fixed statistical assumptions.
  • Less flexible than software-based methods.

Summary

The Krejcie and Morgan method simplifies sample size determination by providing a ready-to-use reference table. Because of its simplicity and reliability, it remains one of the most frequently cited methods in educational research.
