Cochran Formula

Sample Size Determination for Large or Infinite Populations

Introduction

The Cochran Formula is one of the most widely used statistical methods for determining the required sample size when studying large populations. It helps researchers estimate an appropriate sample size that provides reliable results while maintaining a desired level of precision.


Learning Objectives

  • Explain the purpose of the Cochran Formula.
  • Identify the variables used in the formula.
  • Compute the required sample size.
  • Interpret the computed sample size.

Historical Background

William G. Cochran introduced this formula in his work on sampling techniques. The Cochran Formula is especially useful when the population is very large or when the exact population size is unknown.

Today it is widely used in education, healthcare, marketing, public administration, and social science research.


Definition

The Cochran Formula estimates the minimum sample size required to achieve a desired level of confidence and margin of error for studies involving large or infinite populations.


Formula

n0 = (Z2 * p * q) / e2


Variables

Symbol Description
Z Z-score corresponding to the desired confidence level
p Estimated proportion possessing the attribute
q 1 - p
e Margin of error
n0 Required sample size

Common Values Used

Confidence Level Z Value
90% 1.645
95% 1.96
99% 2.576

Worked Example

Given:
  • Z = 1.96
  • p = 0.50
  • q = 0.50
  • e = 0.05

n0 = (1.962 * 0.50 * 0.50) / 0.052

= (3.8416 * 0.25) / 0.0025

= 0.9604 / 0.0025

Required Sample Size = 384 Respondents

Applications

  • Educational research
  • Public health surveys
  • Marketing research
  • Opinion polls
  • Social science research

Advantages

  • Suitable for large populations.
  • Scientifically accepted worldwide.
  • Provides statistically reliable estimates.
  • Applicable to many research fields.

Limitations

  • Requires selecting appropriate values for p and q.
  • Assumes random sampling.
  • May require adjustment for small populations.

Comparison with Taro Yamane Formula

Taro Yamane Cochran
Known finite population Large or unknown population
Simple computation Uses confidence level and population proportion
Requires only N and e Requires Z, p, q, and e

Summary

The Cochran Formula is a powerful and widely accepted method for determining sample size in quantitative research involving large populations. It provides researchers with statistically reliable estimates based on confidence level, population proportion, and margin of error.
